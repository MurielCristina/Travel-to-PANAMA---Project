Why Panama?

Panama
Center of the world, heart of the universe.

Legend has it that Panama City was founded by the Spanish conquistadors who saw its potential as a strategic hub for trade and exploration. Much like other historic capitals, Panama City started as a modest settlement along the coast. However, it wasn't until the early 20th century, with the completion of the Panama Canal, that the city truly flourished, becoming known as 'the crossroads of the world'.

Today, remnants of old Panama City can still be discovered. Amidst the modern skyline adorned with gleaming skyscrapers, you can stroll through the charming streets of Casco Viejo. Here, you'll find beautifully preserved colonial architecture, vibrant plazas, and quaint cafes serving local delicacies like ceviche and empanadas. As you explore, you'll see the fusion of history and modernity, with the majestic Pacific Ocean ever present on the horizon.

In every corner of Panama City, the rich tapestry of its past blends seamlessly with the vibrant energy of the present. The city's diverse cultural influences are reflected in its music, festivals, and the warmth of its people. As you wander through its bustling streets, you'll experience a unique blend of old-world charm and contemporary flair. Whether you're marveling at the engineering marvel of the Panama Canal, savoring the flavors of Panamanian cuisine, or enjoying the lively nightlife, Panama City offers a captivating journey through time and tradition, all set against the backdrop of a stunning coastal landscape.

My favorite brunch in Panama

Tomillo

Offers a diverse menu that blends Panamanian flavors with international cuisine. Their brunch options are a must-try, featuring everything from savory empanadas to sweet tropical fruit bowls.

Address:

Av. B, Casco Viejo

What I like about it

Tomillo's ambiance is perfect for a leisurely brunch. The outdoor seating area is charming, and the menu is filled with delicious and innovative dishes that showcase local ingredients.
